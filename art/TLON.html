THE LION OF
NAUSHERA



Esha Santosh



Of all the things an Indian has to take pride in, be it our diverse culture or our multitalented compatriots, our army men never fail to break glass ceilings and make our hearts swell with pride. The tales of their bravery always inspire us all and instil in each one of us a sense of patriotism and unity. One such tale of an Indian brave-heart is that of Brigadier Usman, a fierce fighter who played a crucial role in the Indo-Pakistan war of 1947-48.


Hailing from a small town in Uttar Pradesh, Brigadier Usman joined the Indian Army in 1934. Upon Partition, he was offered the post of the Chief of Pakistani Army, which he refused without a thought. He left the Baloch regiment to join the ranks of the Indian soldiers and was then stationed at strategic locations of Jammu and Kashmir, namely Naushera and Jhangar. His fierce on-field retaliation towards the attacks in 1948 earned him the title of “The Lion of Naushera”. Upon suffering many casualties at his hands, Pakistan was forced to place a price on the head of the man who they once hoped would give them their best chance at victory.


Brigadier Usman, like all Indian soldiers, did his nation proud and fought bravely, always placing the welfare of the nation and its people above his own. Defending Jhangar in 1948, his last words were, “I am dying, but let not the territory we were fighting for fall to the enemy.” Brigadier Usman wasn’t just a valiant soldier who fought to the end, he was also an incredibly compassionate man who spent a large part of his earnings to pay for the education of the poor.


A man with a thirst for victory and an active contributor to the development of society, Brigadier Usman embodied the virtues of the ideal citizen, inspiring many through tales of his valour. The inspiring officers of our country, both then and now, show us that the true sense of patriotism lies in placing the country above oneself. They teach us that patriotism is exhibited not through aggression or fanaticism, but through discipline and unity. They show us all that patriotism is not in demeaning the enemy, but in reaching the pinnacles of excellence and emerging victorious ourselves – both on the battlefield and off of it.
