INTERVIEW OF
SPORTS CAPTAIN



Sannidhi Krrovidi

Picture credits: Shreyani Kadiyala.

There is very little Shreya Goparaju can’t do! She is the Head Girl for the CBSE section of CHIREC, she is a singer, a fashionista, a basketball player and the person you go to when you want to hear a terrible joke. Her carefree and friendly attitude never fails to bring a smile to your face and has repeatedly proven that if you put your mind to something, there is very little you cannot do. Read on to find out what Shreya has to say about her role as Head Girl, her singing career and more!


1)What do you think have been the most important values you’ve learnt from being so involved in sports?

I think teamwork, discipline, dedication and good communication are the most important things I’ve learnt. Over the years I have also developed a passion for the sports I play and I've learnt how to analyze my opponent so I can turn his/her weakness into my strength.


2) As someone heavily engaged in athletics, who is one athlete you look up to and why?

I look up to Lebron James (Basketball player). He is very hardworking and he made his way to being one of the most respected basketball players from nothing. Lebron didn’t just start with basketball, his main aim when he started playing sports was to get fit, and he continued with basketball because of his passion and skill. When Lebron is on the court, he is dominating and confident which is inspiring.


3) Other than Basketball and Tai Kwon Do, which sports do you like to participate in for fun?

Handball because it’s a rough sport with more flexible rules and it allows one to be more expressive and fluent while playing. It serves as an outlet for stress because of how intense and enjoyable it can be.


4) What would you tell your juniors about sports and its impact on their lives?

I would tell them to take full advantage of the facilities we are provided within Chirec. Academics as important as they are, need to be balanced, and sports and fitness can be utilized as a very effective outlet in doing so. It helps to destress and let loose for a while and keeps you fit. I think that the students shouldn’t neglect sports in the name of studying more or focusing on academics as it helps one have a well-rounded and balanced lifestyle.


5) To you, what’s the best part about competing?

The adrenaline rush that comes with being able to compete, playing a sport you love, is unmatchable. Everything from intimidating your opponent to sparring or being on the court is something that I look forward to every competition.
